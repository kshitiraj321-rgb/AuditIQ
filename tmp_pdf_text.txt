 SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
 Project Overview
 AuditIQ is an AI-powered Exception Intelligence Copilot designed to automate one of the most
time-consuming and error-prone activities in finance, procurement, and audit operations:
manual document reconciliation and exception detection.
 Organizations routinely compare Purchase Orders (POs), Goods Receipt Notes (GRNs),
Invoices, Contracts, Bank Statements, and supporting documentation to identify discrepancies,
overbilling, missing records, duplicate payments, pricing variances, and compliance risks.
 Despite significant investments in ERP systems and workflow software, a substantial portion of
reconciliation and audit work continues to be performed manually using spreadsheets, emails,
and document reviews.
 AuditIQ leverages Artificial Intelligence to automate this process by extracting structured
information from business documents, comparing records across multiple sources, identifying
exceptions, estimating financial impact, assigning risk levels, and generating recommended
actions.
 The result is a dramatic reduction in manual review effort, faster issue identification, improved
audit readiness, and reduced financial exposure.
 Problem Statement
 Finance, procurement, and audit professionals spend a significant amount of time manually
comparing information across multiple business documents to identify inconsistencies and risks.
 Typical workflows require users to:
 ●   Review Purchase Orders
 ●   Verify Goods Receipt Notes
 ●   Compare Vendor Invoices
 ●   Check contract terms
 ●   Identify quantity mismatches
●   Detect pricing discrepancies
 ●   Investigate duplicate invoices
 ●   Estimate financial impact
 ●   Create exception reports
 These activities are often performed using spreadsheets and manual review processes that are:
 ●   Time consuming
 ●   Error prone
 ●   Difficult to scale
 ●   Dependent on individual expertise
 ●   Expensive to maintain
 The core problem is not the lack of data.
 The core problem is that professionals must manually reconcile information across disconnected
documents and systems to identify exceptions and make decisions.
 Proposed Solution
 AuditIQ automates cross-document reconciliation and exception analysis.
 Users upload business documents such as:
 ●   Purchase Orders (PO)
 ●   Goods Receipt Notes (GRN)
 ●   Vendor Invoices
 AuditIQ then:
 1.   Extracts structured information from each document
 2.   Identifies relationships between records
 3.   Compares quantities, prices, and totals
 4.   Detects exceptions and inconsistencies
 5.   Calculates potential financial exposure
 6.   Assigns risk levels
 7.   Generates recommended actions
 8.   Produces an executive summary dashboard
 This transforms a manual review process that may take hours into a workflow that can be
completed within minutes.
Target Users
 Primary Users
 Accounts Payable Analysts
 Responsible for:
 ●   Invoice validation
 ●   Payment verification
 ●   Vendor reconciliation
 Secondary Users
 Procurement Managers
 Responsible for:

 ●   Vendor performance monitoring
 ●   Contract compliance

 Core Value Proposition
 AuditIQ helps organizations answer a critical operational question:
 "Do these documents actually agree with each other?"
 Instead of manually comparing records across multiple files, users receive a prioritized list of
exceptions, risks, and recommended actions.
 AuditIQ converts document review from a manual investigation process into an AI-assisted
decision-making process.

 2.1 The Core Observation
 The modern enterprise does not suffer from a lack of data.
 Organizations already possess vast amounts of information stored across Enterprise Resource
Planning (ERP) systems, procurement platforms, accounting software, spreadsheets, emails,
contracts, invoices, reports, audit records, and operational databases.
 Despite this abundance of information, employees continue to spend significant amounts of time
manually comparing, validating, reconciling, and investigating data before they can make
decisions.
 The problem is not data availability.
 The problem is identifying what requires attention.
 Across finance, procurement, compliance, audit, and operations functions, professionals
repeatedly ask the same questions:
 ●   What is wrong?
 ●   What changed?
 ●   What does not match?
 ●   What requires action?
 ●   What represents risk?
 ●   What should I investigate first?
 Most software systems store information.
 Very few systems explain what the information means.
 This gap creates a large amount of manual work that organizations continue to perform every
day.
 AuditIQ exists to close that gap.

 2.3 Why Existing Software Fails
 Organizations have spent decades investing in software.
 ERP systems manage transactions.
 Accounting systems manage financial records.
 Procurement systems manage purchasing.
 Compliance platforms manage controls.
 Dashboard tools provide visibility.
 Despite these investments, manual reconciliation remains widespread.
 The reason is simple.
 Most software systems are designed to store information and execute workflows.
 They are not designed to reason across multiple sources and explain exceptions.
 Traditional systems answer:
 ●   What happened?
●   When did it happen?
 ●   Who performed the action?
 They often fail to answer:
 ●   Why is this unusual?
 ●   Why does this matter?
 ●   What should happen next?
 As a result, professionals continue exporting information into spreadsheets where they manually
perform comparisons and investigations.
 The existence of sophisticated enterprise software has not eliminated reconciliation work.
 It has simply changed where that work occurs.
 2.4 Why AI Changes Everything
 Historically, automation depended on predefined rules.
 Rules-based systems work well when conditions are predictable.
 For example:
 IF Invoice Amount > Purchase Order Amount
 THEN Flag Exception
 However, real business operations are rarely that simple.
 Documents vary in format.
 Information may be incomplete.
 Relationships may be ambiguous.
 Exceptions often require context.
 Modern AI introduces a new capability:
 Reasoning.
 Instead of simply executing predefined rules, AI can:
 ●   Interpret documents
 ●   Extract meaning
●   Compare information across sources
 ●   Identify inconsistencies
 ●   Explain findings
 ●   Recommend actions
 This represents a fundamental shift.
 The value of AI is not that it can read documents.
 The value of AI is that it can understand relationships between documents.
 That capability makes AI uniquely suited for reconciliation and exception detection workflows.

10.11 Financial Exposure Rules
 Financial Exposure represents the estimated business impact of an identified exception.
 The purpose of Financial Exposure is to quantify potential financial risk and provide context for
risk scoring, recommendations, and explainability.
 ### Quantity Mismatch
 Exposure = Missing Quantity × Unit Price
 Example:
 PO Quantity = 500
 GRN Quantity = 450
 Unit Price = ₹62
 Missing Quantity = 50
 Exposure = ₹3,100
 ### Price Variance
Exposure = Quantity × Price Difference
 ### Missing Invoice
 Exposure = Purchase Order Total Amount
 ### Missing GRN
 Exposure = Invoice Total Amount
 ### Duplicate Invoice
 Exposure = Invoice Total Amount
 ### Guiding Principle
 Financial Exposure should represent the estimated business impact of an exception rather than
simply the arithmetic difference between document totals.
 This ensures consistency across:
 - Risk Assessment
 - Explainability
 - Recommendations
- Dashboard Metrics
